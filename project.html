Math-related projects

Spatial Analysis of robbery and theft crimes in Wuhan city

Team Leader

May,2019 ~ Jan,2020

Overview: This project is listed among A awards (highest level) of national college students' innovation and entrepreneurship program in 2019. The aim of this project is to provide a limited perspective on how geographical factors could potentially be related to the theft and robbery rate regionally in Wuhan City. Through utilizing knowledge and techniques from spatial statistics and geographical data processing, suggestions were given in order to improve the city's early warning system after analyzing the correlations between geographical factors and crime rate.

Analysis, enhancements and suggestions for Google Maps Interface

Core Member

Sep,2021 ~ Dec,2021

Overview: In this report, we focused on suggested improvements that can be made to the Google maps mobile applications to better support the users than its current conditions. Suggestions were given based on knowledge learned from the cognitive ergonomics course. Techniques were employed to facilitate the decision-making process, minimize the information access cost, reduce data cluster, etc..

Evaluating the effects of caffeine intake on memory

Core Member

Jan,2022 ~ Apr,2022

Overview: In this study, we were interested in evaluating how different factors affect working memory, such as caffeine and presentation time. Participants joined the research study by voluntarily signing up through a Google Sheet distributed to them. Twenty-five students enrolled in the IOE 465 course at the University of Michigan were sampled for this experiment. During the signup process, participants were able to denote whether they were able to ingest caffeine.
